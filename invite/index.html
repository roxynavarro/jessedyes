FAQ

Can we stay overnight in the resort? Yes, you are very much welcome to stay during the wedding weekend! Fortunately, we have the resort reserved just for us during this time. Do mention in the RSVP form that you plan to spend more time with us.
How do we get to the venue? If you will be bringing a car, you can follow the directions from Waze or Google Maps. Type in "Anne Raquel's Resort" and you should be good to go! If you are flying in, Clark International Airport is the nearest airport while flying into Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) is also a good option. There used to be shuttles from these airports going to Olongapo City but has temporarily stopped due to the pandemic. As we draw closer to the wedding date, we will be updating you on the available transporation options.
Will parking be available? Yes! We have ample parking for all the guests.
Do you have any gift preferences? We are honestly grateful for the time, money, and effort you'll be spending just to be there on our special day. However, if you really insist, cash gifts will also be most welcome.
Am I allowed to take photos/videos? We would like to have an unplugged wedding. During the church ceremony, kindly turn off your cellphones and gadgets, especially during the bridal march. We have very talented photographers and videographers to document everyhting for us.

Don't worry, you can take all the photos you want during the processional, after the ceremony, and during the reception.
When are the RSVPs due? We completely understand some of you may not be able to confirm your attendance until closer to the date, particularly those who are travelling. However, we will require your RSVP no later than December 5 in order to confirm numbers with our caterer and venue.

If you have any concerns, kindly message Gabby Layugan at +639175488499.
What if I have food allergies or dietary restrictions? We'd love for you to enjoy the food during the reception. Please note any allergies or restrictions in the RSVP form.
Can I bring a plus one? In light of the current situation, unfortunately, guests are not allowed to bring dates as we are trying to manage the total number of guests. We are looking forward to a safe and intimate wedding experience for everyone.
Do we need to be fully vaccinated against COVID-19 to attend the wedding? How about testing requirements? We would like our wedding celebration to be not just fun and joyful, but also safe for all our friends and loved ones. We encourage everyone to get vaccinated as soon as possible in order to protect themselves and the people they interact with. We will also have all our guests and suppliers undergo a COVID-19 test prior to the wedding.
Will live streaming options be available if we can’t attend? The pandemic forced us to limit the number of guests present but we'd love to have as much friends and family celebrate with us, even virtually. We will keep you posted once details are finalized!
What if I have other questions not listed here in the FAQ? Don't fret! You can message Jessa or Edrick through their FB pages.
